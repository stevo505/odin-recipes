Lithuanian Dumplings

Description

Cepelinai, or lithuanian dumplings, are the national dish of Lithuania. They are heart and rich, made using dough, then filled with a pork mixture, and topped with sour cream and bacon bits. These dumplings are known for taking quite some time to make, but the time and effort is well worth it, as the dumplings are also known for being very tasty!

This recipe has been a family staple for generations. It is a main dish that is usually served on a special occasion, like birthdays, holidays, or any kind of family gathering. Try it out with your family!

Ingredients

Dough:

  • 350g (2 1/3 cup) all purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 tsp salt

Filling:

  • 400g ground pork
  • 1 egg
  • 1 small onion
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 2/3 tsp salt
  • 1/3 tbsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp sugar

Steps

Dough:

  1. Mix egg with salt and water, then add flour and mix together. When dough becomes hard to mix, knead by hand.
  2. Add flour slowly and knead knead dough for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add flour until the dough stops sticking to hands, but retains its soft and elastic texture.
  4. Wrap dough in plastic wrap. Let stand for 15 minutes at room temperature.

Filling:

  1. Peel and dice onion. Heat oil in pan, then add onion and fry until softened. Let stand to cool, then mix all ingredients in one bowl.
  2. Take a small piece of dough and roll on into a thin sheet. Squeeze desired size circles using a glass. Place a half tsp of meat filling in the center of each circle.
  3. Fold circles in half, squeezing the edges together and twisting them into a string. Repeat for all dumplings.
  4. Bring water to a boil in a large pot, then add dumplings and boil for 5 minutes.
  5. Serve and ejoy with sour cream and fried bacon bits on top.
