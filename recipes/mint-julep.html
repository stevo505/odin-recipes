Mint Julep

Description

Pair your freshly prepared Monte Cristo sandwich with this delicious New Orleands drink: the Mint Julep! This classic refreshment, the signature drink of the Kentucky derby, combines citrus flavors with minty syrup over crushed ice. This is the perfect drink to cool down in the summertime.

The Mint Julep can be served alcoholic as a bourbon cocktail, but this recipe makes a non-alcoholic variant. This drink is so easy to make and doesn't take too long, so the whole family will have fun making these together!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 2 cups crushed ice
  • 1/2 cup prepared lemonade
  • Fresh mint sprigs to garnish

Steps

  1. In a small saucepan, combine water, sugar, and 1 tablespoon of chopped mint. Stir and bring a boil, cooking until sugar has dissolved. Remove and set aside to cool.
  2. Let stand for about an hour, then strain out mint leaves.
  3. Fill 2 chilled glasses with crushed eyes. Pour half of the lemonade into each glass and top with a splash of sugar syrup.
  4. Garnish each drink with a mint sprig. Serve and enjoy!
